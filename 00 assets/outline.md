## **Core Techniques**

### **1. Source Material**
- **iPhone recordings** – His favorite "microphone." The built-in compression algorithms amplify room noise and ambience, giving recordings a raw, present quality
- **Field recordings** – Street sounds, nature, room tone
- **Found sounds** – Voice notes, conversations, YouTube clips, Instagram audio
- **Dragging finished songs into granular synths** – He starts with complex, intricate sounds and finds order in them (rather than building from pure tones)

### **2. Granular Synthesis** (the secret weapon)
- **Output Portal** is his main tool – lets you process audio in real-time without rendering
- Chops audio into tiny grains (5-200ms), then rearranges/pitch-shifts/time-stretches them
- **Logic's Alchemy** (granular mode) also gets heavy use
- Key: "You can get to really interesting places without really understanding it" – embrace the chaos

### **3. Modulation = Movement**
- **LFOs at different speeds** – Turn off BPM sync and run each modulator at different rates with different shapes
- Modulate: filter cutoff, wavetable position, pitch, volume, panning, reverb dry/wet
- This creates **non-repeating evolution** – the sound never loops back to the same state

### **4. Layering Strategy**
- **2-6 drones per track**, each with different harmonic content
- Combine different sound sources for broader palette
- Layer field recordings subtly underneath for environmental context

### **5. Reverb + Delay for Depth**
- Not just for space—used to **evolve the sound**
- **Reverb drones**: Feed reverb back into itself, automate parameters
- Long decay times wash sounds into the background
- Delay with feedback creates cascading textures

### **6. Brian Eno's Influence**
- Eno gave fred a **file of thousands of drone sounds** to build from
- They **spend hours together** just listening to how drones evolve
- Eno's approach: Create immersive experiences, not just "music"

## **Step-by-Step Approach to Recreate**

### **Starting Point:**
1. **Record source material** on your iPhone (voice notes, ambient sounds, field recordings)
2. **Import into Logic** (or your DAW)
3. **Load into granular synth** (Portal, Alchemy, or similar)

### **Building the Drone:**
1. **Start with 1-2 grains** of your source audio
2. **Modulate grain size, density, pitch** with non-synced LFOs
3. **Add reverb with long decay** (80-100% wet)
4. **Layer 2-3 more drones** from different sources
5. **Automate filter cutoff** for slow movement
6. **Add vinyl noise/distortion** for lo-fi grit

### **Evolution:**
1. **Sit with it** – fred and Eno spend *hours* listening to drones evolve. Let it breathe.
2. **Automate reverb/delay parameters** so the space shifts
3. **Add subtle field recordings** underneath for environment
4. **Sidechain to kick** (if layering with drums) for rhythmic pumping
